**ケムニッツ** (Chemnitz, \[2\]) は、[ドイツ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ドイツ "wikilink")連邦共和国・[ザクセン州](../Page/ザクセン州.md "wikilink")の都市で[独立市](../Page/独立市.md "wikilink")である。2008年までは旧[ケムニッツ行政管区](../Page/ケムニッツ行政管区.md "wikilink")に属していた。人口は約{{\#expr:/10000 round 0}}万人。

[旧東ドイツ](../Page/ドイツ民主共和国.md "wikilink")（[1953年](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/1953年 "wikilink") - [1990年](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990年 "wikilink")）時代には、**カール＝マルクス＝シュタット** (**Karl-Marx-Stadt**) と称されたが、「[ベルリンの壁崩壊](../Page/ベルリンの壁崩壊.md "wikilink")」以降は旧称の「ケムニッツ」に戻された。

## 語源

ケムニッツというめずらしい言葉は、近くの都市[ツヴィッカウ](../Page/ツヴィッカウ.md "wikilink")を流れて最終的には[エルベ川](../Page/エルベ川.md "wikilink")へそそぐツヴィカウア・ムルデ川（[Zwickauer Mulde](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Zwickauer_Mulde "wikilink")）を原住民の[ソルブ語](../Page/ソルブ語.md "wikilink")で「石が多い（小川）」を意味するカミェニカ（Kamjenika）と呼ぶことから来ており、[スラブ語](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/スラブ語 "wikilink")系では「[Kamen](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:ru:Камень "wikilink")」は「[石](../Page/石.md "wikilink")」で、「-ika」は[女性形](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/女性形 "wikilink")[接尾詞](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/接尾詞 "wikilink")である。

## 地勢・産業

[エルツ山地](../Page/エルツ山地.md "wikilink")の山麓辺りに位置し、これを越えると[チェコ](../Page/チェコ.md "wikilink")領に達する。近隣の都市としては、約70キロ北西に[ライプツィヒ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ライプツィヒ "wikilink")、30キロ南西に[ツヴィッカウ](../Page/ツヴィッカウ.md "wikilink")がある。昔はドイツの楽器産業の中心地の一つで、[ケムニッツァ・コンサーティーナや](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/コンサーティーナ#ケムニッツァ・コンサーティーナ "wikilink")[バンドネオン](../Page/バンドネオン.md "wikilink")の発明とも縁の深い都市である。

## 歴史

[thumbの記念碑](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ファイル:Karl-Marx-Monument_in_Chemnitz.jpg "wikilink")\]\] [12世紀](../Page/12世紀.md "wikilink")、[神聖ローマ皇帝](../Page/神聖ローマ皇帝.md "wikilink")[ロタール3世の時代に建てられた](../Page/ロタール3世_\(神聖ローマ皇帝\).md "wikilink")。[17世紀](../Page/17世紀.md "wikilink")の[三十年戦争](../Page/三十年戦争.md "wikilink")で壊滅的な打撃を受けるが、その後復興した。[19世紀](../Page/19世紀.md "wikilink")以降、ドイツで[産業革命](../Page/産業革命.md "wikilink")が進展する中、工業都市として発展した。

[第二次世界大戦](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/第二次世界大戦 "wikilink")後、[1953年](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/1953年 "wikilink")に東ドイツ政府が街を「カール＝マルクス＝シュタット」と改称し（「[カール・マルクス](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/カール・マルクス "wikilink")都市」の意。ただしマルクスとケムニッツは直接関係がない）、[カール＝マルクス＝シュタット県](../Page/カール＝マルクス＝シュタット県.md "wikilink")の県都となった。

[1991年](../Page/1991年.md "wikilink")の[ドイツ再統一](../Page/ドイツ再統一.md "wikilink")後、再び街の名称はケムニッツに戻された。

## Culture and sights

[:en:Theater Chemnitz](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Theater_Chemnitz "wikilink") offers a variety of theatre: opera, plays, ballet and Figuren (puppets), and runs concerts by the orchestra Robert-Schumann-Philharmonie.

Tourist sights include the Kassberg neighborhood with 18th and 19th century buildings and the [:en:Karl Marx Monument](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Karl_Marx_Monument "wikilink") by [:en:Lev Kerbel](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Lev_Kerbel "wikilink"), nicknamed  (a [Saxon dialect](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Upper_Saxon_German "wikilink") word for head) by the locals. Landmarks include the Old Town Hall with its Renaissance portal (15th century), the castle on the site of the former monastery, and the area around the [opera house](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Theater_Chemnitz "wikilink") and the old university. The most conspicuous landmark is the red tower built in the late 12th or early 13th century as part of the [:en:defensive wall](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:defensive_wall "wikilink") (city wall).

The [:en:Chemnitz petrified forest](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Chemnitz_petrified_forest "wikilink") is located in the courtyard of [:en:Kulturkaufhaus Tietz](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Kulturkaufhaus_Tietz "wikilink"). It is one of the very few in existence, and dates back several million years. Also within the city limits, in the district of Rabenstein, is the smallest castle in Saxony, [Rabenstein Castle](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Rabenstein_Castle_\(Saxony\) "wikilink").

The city has changed considerably since German reunification. Most of its industry is now gone and the core of the city has been rebuilt with many shops as well as huge shopping centres. Many of these shops are international brands, including [Zara](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Zara_\(clothing\) "wikilink"), [H\&M](../Page/H&M.md "wikilink"), Esprit,  Galeria Kaufhof, Leiser Shoes, and Peek & Cloppenburg. The large  Galerie Roter Turm (Red Tower) shopping centre is very popular with young people.

The Chemnitz Industrial Museum is an Anchor Point of ERIH, the [:en:European Route of Industrial Heritage](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:European_Route_of_Industrial_Heritage "wikilink"). The State Museum of Archaeology Chemnitz opened in 2014 and is located in the former [:en:Schocken Department Stores](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Schocken_Department_Stores "wikilink").

The [:de:Museum Gunzenhauser](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Museum_Gunzenhauser "wikilink"), formerly a bank, opened on 1 December 2007. [:de:Alfred Gunzenhauser](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Alfred_Gunzenhauser "wikilink"), who lived in Munich, had a collection of some 2,500 pieces of [:en:modern art](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:modern_art "wikilink"), including many paintings and drawings by [:de:Otto Dix](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Otto_Dix "wikilink"), [:de:Karl Schmidt-Rottluff](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Karl_Schmidt-Rottluff "wikilink") and others. The [:de:Botanischer Garten Chemnitz](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Botanischer_Garten_Chemnitz "wikilink") is a municipal [:en:botanical garden](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:botanical_garden "wikilink"), and the [:de:Arktisch-Alpiner Garten der Walter-Meusel-Stiftung](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Arktisch-Alpiner_Garten_der_Walter-Meusel-Stiftung "wikilink") is a non-profit garden specializing in [:en:arctic](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:arctic "wikilink") and [:en:alpine plants](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:alpine_plant "wikilink").

## Urban renewal

[Chemnitz_Stadtzentrum_vom_Kaßberg_2014.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Chemnitz_Stadtzentrum_vom_Kaßberg_2014.jpg "fig:Chemnitz_Stadtzentrum_vom_Kaßberg_2014.jpg")

Heavy destruction in World War II as well as post-war demolition to erect a truly socialist city centre left the city with a vast open space around its [town hall](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:city_and_town_halls "wikilink") where once a vibrant city heart had been. Because of massive investment in out-of-town shopping right after reunification, it was not until 1999 that major building activity was started in the centre. Comparable to [:de:Potsdamer Platz](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Potsdamer_Platz "wikilink") in Berlin, a whole new quarter of the city was constructed in recent years. New buildings include the  [:en:department store](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:department_store "wikilink") by [:de:Helmut Jahn](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Helmut_Jahn "wikilink"),  with a façade by [:de:Hans Kollhoff](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Hans_Kollhoff "wikilink") and Peek & Cloppenburg clothing store by  and Partner.

## Economy

Chemnitz is the largest city of the  urban area and is one of the most important economic areas of Germany's new federal states. Chemnitz had a GDP of about €6.3 billion in 2004. Since about 2000, the city's economy has recorded high annual [GDP growth](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Economic_growth "wikilink") rates; Chemnitz is among the top ten German cities in terms of growth rate. The local and regional economic structure is characterized by medium-sized companies, with the heavy industrial sectors of mechanical engineering, metal processing, and vehicle manufacturing as the most significant industries.

About 100,000 people are employed, of whom about 46,000 commute from other municipalities. 16.3% of employees in Chemnitz have a university or [college degree](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:academic_degree "wikilink"), twice the average rate in Germany.

Chemnitz is crossed by the two motorways ([:de:Autobahn](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Autobahn "wikilink")s) [A4](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bundesautobahn_4 "wikilink") [:de:Erfurt](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Erfurt "wikilink") – [:en:Dresden](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Dresden "wikilink") and [A72](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Bundesautobahn_72 "wikilink") [Hof](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:de:Hof,_Germany "wikilink") – [:en:Leipzig](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Leipzig "wikilink"). ### Airports

Near Chemnitz there are three airports, including the two [:en:international airports](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:international_airport "wikilink") of Saxony in Dresden and Leipzig. Both [:en:Leipzig/Halle Airport](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Leipzig/Halle_Airport "wikilink") and [:en:Dresden Airport](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Dresden_Airport "wikilink") are situated about  from Chemnitz and offer numerous continental as well as intercontinental flights.
