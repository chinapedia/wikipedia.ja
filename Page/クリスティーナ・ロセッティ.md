[thumb](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ファイル:Christina_Rossetti_3.jpg "wikilink")、1848年）\]\] [thumb](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ファイル:Christina_Rossetti_2.jpg "wikilink") **クリスティーナ・ジョージナ・ロセッティ**（Christina Georgina Rossetti, [1830年](../Page/1830年.md "wikilink")[12月5日](../Page/12月5日.md "wikilink") - [1894年](../Page/1894年.md "wikilink")[12月29日](../Page/12月29日.md "wikilink")）は、[イギリス](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/イギリス "wikilink")の詩人。[敬虔主義](../Page/敬虔主義.md "wikilink")的な作風による作品を残した。画家・詩人[ダンテ・ゲイブリエル・ロセッティ](../Page/ダンテ・ゲイブリエル・ロセッティ.md "wikilink")の妹で、兄をはじめとする[ラファエル前派](../Page/ラファエル前派.md "wikilink")の画家たちの作品のモデルにもなった。

人道的な観点から、[奴隷制](../Page/奴隷制.md "wikilink")や[売春](../Page/売春.md "wikilink")による性的搾取、[動物実験](../Page/動物実験.md "wikilink")に反対した。

<td><p><poem style="margin-left:2em;"></p>
<p><strong>The Milking-Maid</strong> (<strong><em>ミルクメイド</em></strong>)</p>
<p>The year stood at its equinox, And bluff the North was blowing. A bleat of lambs came from the flocks, Green hardy things were growing. I met a maid with shining locks, Where milky kine were lowing.</p>
<p>She wore a kerchief on her neck, Her bare arm showed its dimple. Her apron spread without a speck, Her air was frank and simple.</p>
<p>She milked into a wooden pail, And sang a country ditty - An innocent fond lovers' tale, That was not wise nor witty.</p>
<p>She kept in time without a beat, As true as church-bell ringers, Unless she tapped time with her feet, Or squeezed it with her fingers.</p>
<p>I stood a minute out of sight, Stood silent for a minute, To eye the pail, and creamy white The frothing milk within it. </poem></p></td>
<td><p><poem style="margin-left:8em;"> To eye the comely milking maid, Herself so fresh and creamy. “Good day to you!” at last I said, She turned her head to see me. “Good day!” she said with lifted head, Her eyes looked soft and dreamy.</p>
<p>And all the while she milked and milked The grave cow heavy-laden. I've seen grand ladies, plumed and silked, But not a sweeter maiden.</p>
<p>But not a sweeter fresher maid Than this in homely cotton, Whose pleasant face and silky braid I have not yet forgotten.</p>
<p>Perhaps my rose is overblown, Not rosy or too rosy. Perhaps in farmhouse of her own Some husband keeps her cosy. Where I should show a face unknown? - Good bye, my wayside posy! </poem></p></td>
