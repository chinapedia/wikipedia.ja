[Beaux-Arts_de_Carcassonne_-_Portrait_d'André_Chénier_-_Suvée_-_Joconde04400001220.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Beaux-Arts_de_Carcassonne_-_Portrait_d'André_Chénier_-_Suvée_-_Joconde04400001220.jpg "fig:Beaux-Arts_de_Carcassonne_-_Portrait_d'André_Chénier_-_Suvée_-_Joconde04400001220.jpg") **アンドレ・マリ・シェニエ**（, [1762年](../Page/1762年.md "wikilink")[10月30日](../Page/10月30日.md "wikilink") - [1794年](../Page/1794年.md "wikilink")[7月25日](../Page/7月25日.md "wikilink")）は、[フランス革命](../Page/フランス革命.md "wikilink")に関係した[フランス](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/フランス "wikilink")の[詩人](../Page/詩人.md "wikilink")。官能的で情感豊かな詩作によって、[ロマン主義](../Page/ロマン主義.md "wikilink")文学運動の先駆者のひとりに位置付けられている。[恐怖政治](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/恐怖政治 "wikilink")が終わるわずか3日前に、「国家反逆罪」を宣告されて[断頭台](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/断頭台 "wikilink")の露と消えたため、その活動は唐突に終わりを迎えた。[ウンベルト・ジョルダーノ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ウンベルト・ジョルダーノ "wikilink")の[オペラ](../Page/オペラ.md "wikilink")作品「[アンドレア・シェニエ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/アンドレア・シェニエ "wikilink")」などに取り上げられている。弟[ジョゼフは文人](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ジョゼフ・ブレーズ・ド・シェニエ "wikilink")・政治家。

## 生涯

[イスタンブール](../Page/イスタンブール.md "wikilink")の[ガラタ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ガラタ "wikilink")地区（現在の[カラキョイ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/カラキョイ "wikilink")）に[外交官](../Page/外交官.md "wikilink")の三男として生まれる。父親ルイ・シェニエはフランスの[ラングドック](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ラングドック "wikilink")出身で、[レバント](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/レバント "wikilink")方面で20年にわたって[織物](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/織物 "wikilink")[商人](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/商人 "wikilink")として働いた後、イスタンブールでフランス[大使](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/大使 "wikilink")相当の地位に任ぜられていた。[ギリシャ](../Page/ギリシャ.md "wikilink")系の母エリザベト・サンティ＝ロマカは、[19世紀](../Page/19世紀.md "wikilink")の[歴史家](../Page/歴史家.md "wikilink")・[政治家](../Page/政治家.md "wikilink")の[アドルフ・ティエール](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/アドルフ・ティエール "wikilink")の祖母と姉妹であった。

アンドレが3歳のとき、一家は[フランス王国](../Page/フランス王国.md "wikilink")に戻り、父親が[1768年](../Page/1768年.md "wikilink")から[1775年](../Page/1775年.md "wikilink")まで[モロッコ](../Page/モロッコ.md "wikilink")でフランス[領事](../Page/領事.md "wikilink")を務めるものの、家族はその間フランスに留まった。数年間[カルカッソンヌ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/カルカッソンヌ "wikilink")のおばのもとで自由奔放に育った後、[パリ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/パリ "wikilink")の[コレージュ・ド・ナヴァール](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/コレージュ・ド・ナヴァール "wikilink")において、[古典文学](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/古典文学 "wikilink")の[詩](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/詩 "wikilink")の[翻訳家](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/翻訳家 "wikilink")として名を揚げた。

1783年に[ストラスブール](../Page/ストラスブール.md "wikilink")のフランス連隊に士官候補生名簿に登載されるが、新たな経験は間もなく潰えてしまう。年末までにパリに戻り、家族の歓待を受けるとともに、母親の[サロン](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/サロン "wikilink")に足繁く通った洗練された[社交界](../Page/社交界.md "wikilink")の常連、とりわけ[ルブラン・＝パンダール](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ポンス・ドニ・エクシャール・ルブラン "wikilink")、[ラヴォワジエ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ラヴォワジエ "wikilink")、[ルジュール](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ジャン・フランソワ・ルジュール "wikilink")、[ドラや](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/クロード・ジョゼフ・ドラ "wikilink")、少し後には[ダヴィッドらと交際する](../Page/ジャック＝ルイ・ダヴィッド.md "wikilink")。

この頃にはすでに詩人になろうと決めており、当時の[新古典主義](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/新古典主義 "wikilink")に参加しようと決心していた。特に、[1784年](../Page/1784年.md "wikilink")に[ローマ](../Page/ローマ.md "wikilink")や[ナポリ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ナポリ "wikilink")、[ポンペイ](../Page/ポンペイ.md "wikilink")を訪れたことから、強く刺激された。

ほぼ3年の間シェニエは、家族からプレッシャーや干渉を受けることなく、詩作を研究し試作に向けた。 この時期、[テオクリトス](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/テオクリトス "wikilink")や[ビオーン](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ビオーン "wikilink")、[古代ギリシャ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/古代ギリシャ "wikilink")の[詞花集を大幅に模倣し](../Page/アンソロジー.md "wikilink")、主に[田園詩や](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/アイディル "wikilink")[牧歌を書いた](../Page/パストラル.md "wikilink")。この頃に書かれたか、少なくとも[スケッチ](../Page/スケッチ.md "wikilink")された詩に、「 *L'Oaristys* 」「 *L'Aveugle* 」「 *La Jeune Malode* 」「 *Bacchus* 」「*Euphrosine* 」「 *La Jeune Tarentine* 」があり、古代神話と、個人の感情や精神という感覚を融合させた。

田園詩や[エレジー](../Page/エレジー.md "wikilink")以外にも、[教訓詩や](../Page/教訓主義.md "wikilink")[哲学](../Page/哲学.md "wikilink")詩の詩作にも挑戦し,[1783年](../Page/1783年.md "wikilink")に*Hermes*の述作を始めた時には、いくらか[ルクレティウス](../Page/ルクレティウス.md "wikilink")流に、[ドゥニ・ディドロ](../Page/ドゥニ・ディドロ.md "wikilink")の[百科全書](../Page/百科全書.md "wikilink")を凝縮して長編詩にすることを目指していた。

現存しているのは断片のみであるが、この詩は、[宇宙](../Page/宇宙.md "wikilink")における[人間](../Page/人間.md "wikilink")の立場について触れているもので、始めは孤立した状態として、そして次に社会的な状態として扱っている\[1\]。また別の断片である"L'Invention"には、シェニエの詩歌に対する考え方が述べられている\[2\]。

1787年に一家の友人であったアンヌ＝セザール・ド・ラ・リュゼルヌが駐英フランス大使に任ぜられた際、シェニエはその秘書に指名され、3年間ロンドンに滞在することになる。だがその生活は必ずしも幸福ではなかった。イギリスではミルトンとジェームズ・トムソンに興味を示し、いくつかの自作の詩にはシェイクスピアやトマス・グレイの影響が見られるとはいえ、英文学を学んだというには程遠かった。

The events of 1789 and the startling success of his younger brother, [Marie-Joseph](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Chénier "wikilink"), as political [playwright](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/playwright "wikilink") and [pamphleteer](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pamphleteer "wikilink"), concentrated all his thoughts upon France. In April 1790 he could stand London no longer, and once more joined his parents at Paris in the rue de Cléry. France was on the verge of [anarchy](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/anarchy "wikilink"). A strong believer in [constitutional monarchism](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/constitutional_monarchism "wikilink"), Chénier believed that the [French Revolution](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Revolution "wikilink") was already complete and that all that remained to be done was the inauguration of the reign of law. Though his political viewpoint was moderate, his tactics were dangerously aggressive: he abandoned his gentle idyls to write poetical [satire](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/satire "wikilink")s. His prose "Avis au peuple Français" (24 August 1790) was followed by the rhetorical "Jeu de paume", a somewhat declamatory moral ode addressed to the painter Jacques-Louis David.

In the meantime he orated at the [Feuillant](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feuillant "wikilink")s Club, and contributed frequently to the *Journal de Paris* from November 1791 to July 1792, when he wrote his scorching [iamb](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/iamb "wikilink")s to [Jean Marie Collot d'Herbois](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Marie_Collot_d'Herbois "wikilink"), *Sur les Suisses révoltés du regiment de Châteauvieux*. The [insurrection](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/10th_of_August_\(French_Revolution\) "wikilink") of 10 August 1792 uprooted his party, his paper and his friends, and he only escaped the [September Massacres](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/September_Massacres "wikilink") by staying with relatives in [Normandy](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Normandy "wikilink"). In the month following these events his brother, Marie-Joseph, had entered the anti-monarchical [National Convention](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Convention "wikilink"). André raged against all these events, in such poems as *Ode à [Charlotte Corday](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlotte_Corday "wikilink")* congratulating France that "Un scélérat de moins rampe dans cette fange." At the request of [Malesherbes](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillaume-Chrétien_de_Lamoignon_de_Malesherbes "wikilink"), the defense counsel to King [Louis XVI](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_XVI "wikilink"), Chénier provided some arguments to the king's defense.

After the king's execution he sought a secluded retreat on the [Plateau de Satory](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plateau_de_Satory "wikilink") at [Versailles](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Versailles "wikilink") and only went out after nightfall. There he wrote the poems inspired by Fanny (Mme Laurent Lecoulteux), including the exquisite "Ode à Versailles". His solitary life at Versailles lasted nearly a year. On 7 March 1794 he was arrested at the house of Mme Piscatory at [Passy](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passy "wikilink"). Two obscure agents of the [Committee of Public Safety](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Committee_of_Public_Safety "wikilink") (one of them named Nicolas Guénot) were in search of a marquise who had fled, but an unknown stranger was found in the house and arrested on suspicion of being the aristocrat they were searching for. This was Chénier, who had come on a visit of sympathy.

He was taken to the [Luxembourg Palace](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luxembourg_Palace "wikilink") and afterwards to Saint-Lazare. During the 140 days of his imprisonment he wrote a series of iambs denouncing the Convention (in alternate lines of 12 and 8 syllables), which, in the words of the 1911 *Encyclopædia Britannica*, "hiss and stab like poisoned bullets", and which were smuggled to his family by a jailer. In prison he also composed his most famous poem, "Jeune captive", a poem at once of enchantment and of despair. Ten days before Chénier's death, the painter [Joseph-Benoît Suvée](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph-Benoît_Suvée "wikilink") completed the well-known portrait of him.

Chénier might have been overlooked but for the well-meant, indignant officiousness of his father. Marie-Joseph did his best to prevent his brother's execution, but he could do nothing more. [Maximilien Robespierre](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximilien_Robespierre "wikilink"), who was himself in dangerous straits, remembered Chénier as the author of the venomous verses in the *Journal de Paris* and sentenced him to death. Chénier was one of the last persons executed by Robespierre.

At sundown, Chénier was taken by cart to the guillotine at what is now the [Place de la Nation](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Place_de_la_Nation "wikilink"). He was executed along with a Princess of Monaco, on a bogus charge of conspiracy. Three days later Robespierre was seized and executed without trial, ending the Terror. Chénier, aged 31 at his execution, was interred in the [Cimetière de Picpus](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cimetière_de_Picpus "wikilink").

The record of Chénier's last moments by [Henri de Latouche](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_de_Latouche "wikilink") is rather melodramatic and is certainly not above suspicion.

## Works

During Chénier's lifetime only his *Jeu de paume* (1791) and *Hymne sur les Suisses* (1792) had been published. For the most part, then, his reputation rests on his posthumously published work, retrieved from oblivion page by page.

The *Jeune Captive* appeared in the *Decade philosophique*, on 9 January 1795; *La Jeune Tarentine* in the *Mercure* of 22 March 1801. [François-René de Chateaubriand](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/François-René_de_Chateaubriand "wikilink") quoted three or four passages in his *Genie du Christianisme*. [Fayette](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fayette "wikilink") and [Lefeuvre-Deumier](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lefeuvre-Deumier "wikilink") also gave a few fragments; but it was not until 1819 that an attempt was made by [Henri de Latouche](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_de_Latouche "wikilink") to collect the poems in a substantive volume. Many more poems and fragments were discovered after Latouche's publication, and were collected in later editions. Latouche also wrote an account of Chénier's last moments, which the 1911 *Encyclopædia Britannica* described as "melodramatic and certainly not above suspicion."

Critical opinions of Chénier have varied wildly. In 1828, [Charles Augustin Sainte-Beuve](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Augustin_Sainte-Beuve "wikilink") praised Chénier as an heroic forerunner of the Romantic movement and a precursor of [Victor Hugo](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Hugo "wikilink"). Chénier, he said, had "inspired and determined" [Romanticism](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanticism "wikilink"). Many other critics also wrote about Chénier as modern and proto-Romantic. However, [Anatole France](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatole_France "wikilink") contests Sainte-Beuve's theory: he claims that Chénier's poetry is one of the last expressions of 18th-century classicism. His work should not be compared to Hugo and the [Parnassian](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parnassianism "wikilink") poets, but to [philosophes](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/philosophes "wikilink") like [André Morellet](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/André_Morellet "wikilink"). [Paul Morillot](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Morillot "wikilink") has argued that judged by the usual test of 1820s Romanticism (love for strange literature of the North, medievalism, novelties and experiments), Chénier would have been excluded from Romantic circles. On the other hand, the ennui and melancholy of his poetry recalls Romanticism, and he experimented in French verse to a much greater extent than other 18th-century poets.

The poet [José María de Heredia](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/José_María_de_Heredia "wikilink") held Chénier in great esteem, saying "I do not know in the French language a more exquisite fragment than the three hundred verses of the *Bucoliques*" and agreeing with Sainte-Beuve's judgment that Chénier was a poet ahead of his time. Chénier has been very popular in Russia, where [Alexandr Pushkin](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandr_Pushkin "wikilink") wrote a poem about his last hours and [Ivan Kozlov](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Kozlov "wikilink") translated *La Jeune Captive*, *La Jeune Tarentine* and other famous pieces. Chénier has also found favor with English-speaking critics; for instance, his love of nature and of political freedom has been compared to [Shelley](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Percy_Bysshe_Shelley "wikilink"), and his attraction to Greek art and myth recalls [Keats](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Keats "wikilink").

Chénier's fate has become the subject of many plays, pictures and poems, notably in the opera *[Andrea Chénier](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrea_Chénier "wikilink")* by [Umberto Giordano](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umberto_Giordano "wikilink"), the epilogue by [Sully-Prudhomme](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/René_François_Armand_Sully-Prudhomme "wikilink"), the *Stello* by [Alfred de Vigny](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_de_Vigny "wikilink"), the delicate statue by Puech in the Luxembourg, and the well-known portrait in the centre of the "Last Days of the Terror."
