**ナポリ料理**にはアラゴンやフランスの場合のように[ナポリ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ナポリ "wikilink")や[王国を支配した異なる文化の影響により何世紀にもわたって豊かにした](../Page/ナポリ王国.md "wikilink")[古代ギリシャ・ローマ世界](../Page/古代ギリシャ・ローマ世界.md "wikilink")時代に遡る古代の歴史的な根源がある。

ナポリが[ナポリ王国](../Page/ナポリ王国.md "wikilink")の首都であったことから農村部の材料（[パスタ](../Page/パスタ.md "wikilink")、野菜、[チーズ](../Page/チーズ.md "wikilink")）や海鮮料理（[魚肉](../Page/魚肉.md "wikilink")、[甲殻類](../Page/甲殻類.md "wikilink")、[軟体動物](../Page/軟体動物.md "wikilink")）の間を取り持ちながらナポリの料理は、[カンパニア州](../Page/カンパニア州.md "wikilink")全域の料理の伝統から多くを取り入れた。膨大なレシピは、**[ティンバッロ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ティンバッロ "wikilink")**や非常に入念に調理したパスタや米の料理****、****（豆入りパスタ）などの野菜入りパスタ料理のように高くないが栄養学的に健康な食材で調理する大衆の伝統から来る料理のように地元の貴族の料理に影響されている。

## 歴史的背景

[Fish_plate_Antikensammlung_Berlin_1984.57.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Fish_plate_Antikensammlung_Berlin_1984.57.jpg "fig:Fish_plate_Antikensammlung_Berlin_1984.57.jpg")の赤く彩られた魚をモチーフにした皿：紀元前340年\]\]

ナポリにはナポリ自体がローマなどの世界のその地域の他の多くの都市に先行する何世紀にもわたる歴史がある。ギリシャやローマ、ゴート族、東ローマ帝国、フランスやスペインの数十人の王の継承者の支配を持ちこたえてきた。それぞれの文化は、食事がナポリやカンパニア州自体で調理される方法に足跡を残した。

[Fishes2.JPG](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Fishes2.JPG "fig:Fishes2.JPG")で発見された[モザイク](../Page/モザイク.md "wikilink")の魚：\]\] [Pompejanischer_Maler_um_70_001.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pompejanischer_Maler_um_70_001.jpg "fig:Pompejanischer_Maler_um_70_001.jpg")のフレスコ画\]\]

現代の料理の伝統と古代ギリシャ・ローマの料理の伝統の間の関係を見出すのは、常に容易ではない。古典的な料理の嗜好の痕跡に[マグナ・グラエキア](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/マグナ・グラエキア "wikilink")（南イタリア）で発見されるギリシャが支配した時代の皿は、海鮮料理がこの時代に重宝された証拠である魚や軟体動物を描いている。[ポンペイ](../Page/ポンペイ.md "wikilink")のフレスコ画は、[イチジク](../Page/イチジク.md "wikilink")や[ザクロ](../Page/ザクロ.md "wikilink")で満たされたフルーツバスケットを描いている。のの遺跡から未知の食材であるケーキのフレスコ画が出土している。

[Pompei_pane.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pompei_pane.jpg "fig:Pompei_pane.jpg")で発見された炭化したパン\]\] [Stanzione,_Massimo_-_Woman_in_Neapolitan_Costume_-_1635.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Stanzione,_Massimo_-_Woman_in_Neapolitan_Costume_-_1635.jpg "fig:Stanzione,_Massimo_-_Woman_in_Neapolitan_Costume_-_1635.jpg") [Makkaroni-4.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Makkaroni-4.jpg "fig:Makkaroni-4.jpg")\]\]

ローマの[ガルムは](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ガルム_\(調味料\) "wikilink")、に特有の現代のに用いられる物に最も近い古代のソースである。**ピッツァディスカロラ**（エンダイブパイ）や**ブラチオレアルラグー**（ソースのミートロール）のように塩辛い料理に[レーズン](../Page/レーズン.md "wikilink")を使いながら[アピシウス](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/アピシウス "wikilink")が表現したローマの料理に特有の甘酸っぱい味覚に由来するとすることができる。[復活祭](../Page/復活祭.md "wikilink")に特有の現代の****ケーキに[コムギ](../Page/コムギ.md "wikilink")を使うのは、元々頃に祝う[アルテミス](../Page/アルテミス.md "wikilink")や[キュベレー](../Page/キュベレー.md "wikilink")、[ケレース](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ケレース "wikilink")の礼賛や多産の祝いに関連する象徴的な意味があったかもしれない。クリスマスケーキである****という名前は、（「丸形」を意味する）ギリシャ語στρόγγυλοςから来ている。

ナポリのスペインとフランスの王家は、貴族階級の料理と貧困階級の料理の違いに着手した。前者は精巧で更に国際的な料理や肉などの大量の高価な食材で彩られた。貧困階級は安くて地元で育った食品（穀物や野菜）を用いた。元々の質素な食材を保存する一方で、今日貧困階級の伝統的なレシピが良い品質と味覚を手に入れるために、このことは幾世紀にもわたって彩られ、貴族階級の料理の影響に触れることになった。

## 典型的な食材

### パスタ

[Sommer,_Giorgio_(1834-1914)_-_n._6204_-_Napoli_-_Fabbrica_di_maccheroni.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Sommer,_Giorgio_\(1834-1914\)_-_n._6204_-_Napoli_-_Fabbrica_di_maccheroni.jpg "fig:Sommer,_Giorgio_(1834-1914)_-_n._6204_-_Napoli_-_Fabbrica_di_maccheroni.jpg")

多彩なナポリの[パスタ](../Page/パスタ.md "wikilink")がある。パスタはナポリで発明された物ではないが、最高品質の一つは、首都から数キロの[グラニャーノ](../Page/グラニャーノ.md "wikilink")とその近郊で発見されている。乾燥し保存する技術と共にパスタの工場生産が始まったのもここであった。主要な食材は、柔らかい小麦より扱いが難しい[デュラムコムギ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/デュラムコムギ "wikilink")であるために、家で作るパスタが一般的な北イタリアより工場生産は大きな成功を収めた。柔らかいパスタに寛容でない一方でナポリでは伝統的にパスタは「**固ゆで**」でなければならない。

[スパゲッティ](../Page/スパゲッティ.md "wikilink")や[リングイネ](../Page/リングイネ.md "wikilink")に加えて最も大衆的なパスタは、料理前に手で割り普通ナポリタンラグーにトッピングする長いパイプ型のパスタ****や****である。野菜付きのパスタも、現在別個の種類のパスタとして工場で生産されたが異なる種類のパスタの一部として作られ嘗ては安く売られた**パスタミスタ**（[ナポリ語](../Page/ナポリ語.md "wikilink")で*pasta ammescata*）と共に通常調理されている。

[穀粉](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/穀粉 "wikilink")やジャガイモと共に調理する手作りの**[ニョッキ](../Page/ニョッキ.md "wikilink")**は、ジャガイモに対するナポリ人の高慢さに打ち勝つ大衆的な方法になっている。1949年、[W・H・オーデン](../Page/W・H・オーデン.md "wikilink")はイスキアのフォリオから[イーゴリ・ストラヴィンスキー](../Page/イーゴリ・ストラヴィンスキー.md "wikilink")に「フォリオは我々がみすぼらしい貧乏人の印とするジャガイモを食べることから狂人と思っている」と書いた。このことを伝えて長くナポリに住むは、「ジャガイモの代わりに殆どチーズやハムなどの食材が圧倒している」フランス風の**ガットー**について述べている\[2\]。****のように現代風にアレンジされたパスタにも大衆化してきている物がある。

### トマト

[Tomate_mit_Grünkragen.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Tomate_mit_Grünkragen.jpg "fig:Tomate_mit_Grünkragen.jpg")の品種\]\] トマトは18世紀にナポリの料理の仲間入りをした。トマトを保存する産業は、有名な**ペラティ**（皮をむいたトマト）や**コンセントラト**（トマトペースト）の世界のあらゆる地域への輸出の結果として19世紀のナポリで始まった。トマトの保存には瓶詰めトマトジュースや刻みトマトといった方法が伝統的に数種類ある。有名な**コンセルヴァ**（天日干しの濃縮ジュース）トマトは、長時間調理し、滑らかな歯応えのある濃い赤色のクリームになる。

### 野菜

**[パルミジャーナ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/パルミジャーナ "wikilink")**（[ナス](../Page/ナス.md "wikilink")パイ）や**ペペロニリピエニ**（詰め込んだ胡椒）のように野菜を使うカンパニア州の料理には正にテーブルの花形となり得るものがある。最も特徴的な製品に[ミネストラ・マリタータ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ミネストラ・マリタータ "wikilink")を調理するのに使う****（地元の多彩な**[ブラッシカ・ラパ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ブラッシカ・ラパ "wikilink")**）、**[エンダイブ](../Page/エンダイブ.md "wikilink")**、二種類の[エンダイブ](../Page/エンダイブ.md "wikilink")、数種類の[ブロッコリー](../Page/ブロッコリー.md "wikilink")、（、様々な****）などがある。異なる種類の[豆](../Page/豆.md "wikilink")や[ヒヨコマメ](../Page/ヒヨコマメ.md "wikilink")などの[豆果](../Page/豆果.md "wikilink")は、非常に大衆的である。

[ズッキーニは広く用いられていて](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Zucchini "wikilink")、一番の例は、[酢](../Page/酢.md "wikilink")や新鮮な[ミント](../Page/ミント.md "wikilink")で揚げるものである。ズッキーニの雄花は、塩入練り粉の中で揚げることができる（****）\[3\]。

普通の赤い胡椒や黄色い胡椒は、広く用いられていて、地元の多彩な小さな緑色の（辛くない）胡椒**ペペロンチーニヴェルディ**は、通常揚げ物に使われる。

[サラダ](../Page/サラダ.md "wikilink")は多くの料理、特に海鮮料理の添え物である。多くのパリッとした[レタス](../Page/レタス.md "wikilink")は、[ニンジン](../Page/ニンジン.md "wikilink")や[ウイキョウ](../Page/ウイキョウ.md "wikilink")、（嘗ては自生し、余り見栄えのしない**[スベリヒユ](../Page/スベリヒユ.md "wikilink")**と売られた）、今日では非常に珍しくなりほぼ完全に丸い甘くなったものに置き換わった伝統的に長く辛いと混ぜ合わせる。

ナポリ料理で使われる黒[オリーブ](../Page/オリーブ.md "wikilink")は、常に[ガエータ](../Page/ガエータ.md "wikilink")産である。

第二次世界大戦中に貧しい家庭で余り魅惑的でない食材を使うのは珍しいことではなかった。[ソラマメ](../Page/ソラマメ.md "wikilink")や[エンドウ](../Page/エンドウ.md "wikilink")の中身のないさやで料理したパスタの調理法が報告されている\[4\]。

### チーズ

[Mozzarella_di_bufala3.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Mozzarella_di_bufala3.jpg "fig:Mozzarella_di_bufala3.jpg")\]\]

柔らかいチーズ、年季の入ったチーズ共にイタリアの料理の重要な要素で、ナポリ料理においてもその立場を保持している。一部の調理法は、非常に古いローマの伝統に由来している。最も新鮮なものから初めて、使われるものは大体以下の通りである。

  - ****：非常に新鮮で軽く、元々手製のかごで売られていた。一般に一部のパスタの具として現在使われている。
  - **[リコッタ](../Page/リコッタ.md "wikilink")フレスカ**：新鮮なまま食べたり添え物の食材として用いたりする。（例えばナポリタンラグーと共に[パスタ](../Page/パスタ.md "wikilink")のトッピングとして）
  - ****：塩辛くいささか年季が入っていて、復活祭に特有のものである。
  - 非常に上品な味わいの[ソレント](../Page/ソレント.md "wikilink")の半島の**カチョッテッラフレスカ**
  - **[モッツァレッラ・ディ・ブーファラ・カンパーナ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/モッツァレッラ・ディ・ブーファラ・カンパーナ "wikilink")**：水牛の牛乳から作り[アヴェルサ](../Page/アヴェルサ.md "wikilink")地区と川の平原で殆ど生産される新鮮なチーズ
  - **[モッツァレッラ](../Page/モッツァレッラ.md "wikilink")**：乳牛の牛乳から作られ、[アジェーロラ](../Page/アジェーロラ.md "wikilink")地区で作られるものが最高である。
  - **[プロヴォローネ](../Page/プロヴォローネ.md "wikilink")アッフミカタ**：外は薄茶色に中は黄色っぽくオーク材でいぶして香りを付けた**フィオルディラッテ**
  - **ボッコンチーニデルカディナレ**または**ブッリエッリ**：クリームか牛乳に浸した粘土製の鍋で調理する小さなモッツァレッラ
  - **[スカモルツァ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/スカモルツァ "wikilink")**：白または燻製
  - **ブッリニディソレント**：[バター](../Page/バター.md "wikilink")入りの小さなプロヴォローネチーズ
  - **[プロヴォローネ](../Page/プロヴォローネ.md "wikilink")**：異なる年代の**[カチョカヴァッロ](../Page/カチョカヴァッロ.md "wikilink")**

### 海鮮料理

[Gianchetti_and_poutine.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Gianchetti_and_poutine.jpg "fig:Gianchetti_and_poutine.jpg")

ナポリ料理は[ティレニア海](../Page/ティレニア海.md "wikilink")の大量のあらゆる種類の海鮮料理を常に使ってきた。ジョンソン博士の友人は、「帝国の豪勢な美味であるや墨を吐く魚と比べて私がこれまでに食べた中で最も素晴らしく最も類い稀で際だって高い風味のある魚（タイセイヨウサバのように大きな）」に熱狂していた\[5\]。がパリ駐在のナポリ大使付の書記官として送られた1759年、普段の食事に悲嘆し、「果物がなく、チーズがなく、良い海鮮料理がない、ここの全てがナポリ気質にとって侵害になっている」ことに気付いた\[6\]。調理には**ズッパ**を調理するのに使う魚のように余り高くない魚特に[カタクチイワシ科](../Page/カタクチイワシ科.md "wikilink")などの魚（現在養殖場から主に売られている**スピゴラ**（[ヨーロピアンシーバス](../Page/ヨーロピアンシーバス.md "wikilink")）や**オラテ**（[ヨーロッパヘダイ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ヨーロッパヘダイ "wikilink")）あるいは**デンティチェ**（**[ヨーロッパキダイ](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ヨーロッパキダイ "wikilink")**）や**サラゴ**（****）、**ペッツォーニャ**（****）のような**スコルファノ**（**[フサカサゴ属](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/フサカサゴ属 "wikilink")**）や**トラチナ**（****）**クオッチオ**（****）または中程度から大きな魚）を使う。非常に小さな魚も使われる。

  - **チチニエッリ**（赤ん坊の魚）：非常に小さく透明で、蒸すか練り粉で揚げて調理する。
  - **フラヴァリオ**：数センチメートルで、主に**トリリア**（****）や**レトゥンニ**（****）の魚で、主として揚げる。

北欧の海から輸入したやは、揚げたりジャガイモやトマトと料理する。

殆どの[頭足類](../Page/頭足類.md "wikilink")（[タコ](../Page/タコ.md "wikilink")、、[コウイカ目](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/コウイカ目 "wikilink")）は、[甲殻類](../Page/甲殻類.md "wikilink")（主に[エビ](../Page/エビ.md "wikilink")）同様に利用されている。

Shellfish *cozze* ([:en:mussel](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:mussel "wikilink")s), *vongole* (clams), *cannolicchi* (*[:en:Ensis](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Ensis "wikilink") [siliqua](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Ensis_siliqua "wikilink")*, [taratufi](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Venus_verrucosa "wikilink"), *telline* (*[:en:Donax trunculus](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Donax_trunculus "wikilink")*), *sconcigli* (*[:en:Haustellum brandaris](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Haustellum_brandaris "wikilink")*)) are employed in many seafood meals, and sometimes are eaten raw, but this happens more and more seldom nowadays. Clams require a special note. The *vongola verace* is *[:en:Venerupis](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Venerupis "wikilink") [decussata](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Venerupis_decussata "wikilink")*, not to be confused with the Philippines clam (*[:en:Venerupis philippinarum](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Venerupis_philippinarum "wikilink")*), very frequently found on the markets, and often called *verace* in northern Italy's markets), and the *lupino* (*[:en:Dosinia](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Dosinia "wikilink") [exoleta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Dosinia_exoleta "wikilink")*).

It is now forbidden by law to sell and eat the *sea dates* (*datteri di mare*, *[:en:Lithophaga](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Lithophaga "wikilink") [lithophaga](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Lithophaga_lithophaga "wikilink")*), as their fishing seriously damages coastline rocks, mainly in the [:en:Sorrento](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/:en:Sorrento "wikilink") peninsula.

### Meat

Meat is not used as frequently in Neapolitan cooking as in the cuisine of Northern Italy. The most common kinds of meat used in Neapolitan cooking are:

  - [sausage](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/sausage "wikilink"): *salsicce* and **, with not finely hand-cut meat (*a ponta 'e curtiello*)
  - pork liver, rounded in a net of pork's fat and a [bay leaf](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/bay_leaf "wikilink")
  - *[trippa](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/tripe "wikilink")* (tripe) and other more humble cuts of [pork](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pork "wikilink") or [beef](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/beef "wikilink"), like the typical ** (pork's foot and cow's nose), and the **, a spicy soup with tomato and hot [chili pepper](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chili_pepper "wikilink").
  - *braciole*, pork rolls stuffed with [raisin](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/raisin "wikilink")s, [pine nuts](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pine_nut "wikilink") and [parsley](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/parsley "wikilink"), fixed with [toothpick](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/toothpick "wikilink")s and cooked in [ragù](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/neapolitan_ragù "wikilink")
  - [lamb](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton "wikilink") and [goat](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/goat "wikilink") are [roasted](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/roasting "wikilink"), usually with potatoes and peas, typically around Easter
  - [rabbit](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/rabbit "wikilink") and [chicken](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chicken "wikilink"), often cooked *alla cacciatora*, [pan fried](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_frying "wikilink") with [tomato](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/tomato "wikilink")es
  - [beef](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/beef "wikilink") or other red meat with [tomato](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/tomato "wikilink")es, cooked for a long time to tenderize an inexpensive piece of meat as in [Carne Pizzaiola](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carne_Pizzaiola "wikilink")

## Bread

Also used are *[sfilatini](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/sfilatino "wikilink")*, somewhat similar to a French [baguette](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/baguette "wikilink"), but shorter and thicker. *Rosetta* rolls and other varieties are also present.

## Pasta dishes

From the classic *"pummarola"* (tomato sauce) to the simplest *aglio e uoglio* ([garlic](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/garlic "wikilink") and [oil](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/oil "wikilink")), down to a wide variety of sauces, with vegetables or seafood, up to the [ragù](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neapolitan_ragù "wikilink"), southern Italy's creativity enhances its [pasta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pasta "wikilink") dishes.

### Pasta dishes of the poor

Cuisine traditionally attributed to the poor often mixes pasta with [legume](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/legume "wikilink")s. The most popular are: *[pasta e fagioli](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pasta_e_fagioli "wikilink")* (pasta with [bean](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/bean "wikilink")s), sometimes enriched with pork rind (*cotiche*), *pasta e ceci* (pasta with [chickpea](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chickpea "wikilink")s), *pasta e lenticchie* (pasta with [lentil](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/lentil "wikilink")s), *pasta e piselli* (pasta with [pea](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pea "wikilink")s). Nowadays *cicerchie* (*[Lathyrus sativus](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lathyrus_sativus "wikilink")*) have become very rare. Similarly to [legume](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/legume "wikilink")s, other vegetables are associated with pasta, like *pasta e patate* (pasta with [potato](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/potato "wikilink")es), *pasta e cavolfiore* (pasta with [cauliflower](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cauliflower "wikilink")), *pasta e zucca* (pasta with [pumpkin](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pumpkin "wikilink")). The most traditional cooking method consists in cooking the condiments first, for instance, [pan fry](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pan_frying "wikilink") [garlic](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/garlic "wikilink") with [oil](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/oil "wikilink"), then add steamed beans, or fry [onion](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/onion "wikilink") and [celery](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/celery "wikilink"), then add potatoes cut into little dices; then, after frying, water is added, brought to boiling temperature, salted, and pasta is added and stirred frequently. While cooking with all the other ingredients, pasta does not lose its [starch](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/starch "wikilink"), which would have been lost if cooked separately in salty water and then drained. Cooking pasta together with vegetables makes the sauce creamier (*"azzeccato"*), and is a way of preparing pasta distinct from the tradition of "noble" cuisine, which prepares similar dishes in a way more similar to broth or soups, adding pasta after cooking it separately. One more hearty dish in the cuisine of the poor is pasta simply cooked with cheese and eggs *stracciatella* (*pasta caso e ova*).

[Spaghetti](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaghetti "wikilink"), dressed with tomato sauce, black olives from [Gaeta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaeta "wikilink") and [caper](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/caper "wikilink")s are called *[spaghetti alla puttanesca](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/spaghetti_alla_puttanesca "wikilink")*. An imaginative recipe was created on the tables of the poor, where the expensive shellfishes were missing: spaghetti, dressed with cherry tomatoes sauce, garlic, oil and parsley are called *spaghetti alle vongole fujute* (spaghetti with *escaped* clams), where clams are present only in the imagination of the people eating the dish.

### Frittata with spaghetti

The [frittata](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/frittata "wikilink") can be prepared with pasta leftovers, either with tomato sauce or white. Pasta, cooked *al dente* is mixed with raw scrambled egg and cheese, then pan fried. It can be enriched with many different ingredients. Must be cooked on both sides, flipped with the help of a plate. If well cooked, it is compact, and can be cut into slices. It can be eaten during outdoor lunches.

### Richer pasta dishes

The aristocratic cuisine used pasta for elaborate recipes, like the *timballi*, rarely used in everyday food.

Richer sauces, more elaborate than the vegetable pasta dishes mentioned above, that are frequently used to dress pasta include:

  - The *Bolognese* sauce, vaguely inspired by the [ragù emiliano](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolognese_sauce "wikilink"), prepared with minced [carrot](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/carrot "wikilink") and [onion](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/onion "wikilink"), ground beef and tomato
  - The *[Genovese sauce](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genovese_sauce "wikilink")*, not inspired by [Genoa](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genoa "wikilink") in spite of the name, but prepared with meat browned with abundant onions and other aromatics

With the Neapolitan ragù the most traditionally used pasta are the [ziti](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ziti "wikilink"), long macaroni, that are broken into shorter pieces by hand before cooking. The *[Neapolitan ragù](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neapolitan_ragù "wikilink")* is also used, together with [fiordilatte](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/fiordilatte "wikilink"), to dress the **, then cooked in oven in a small single-portion clay pot (*pignatiello*).

### Seafood pasta dishes

[Spaghetti_alle_vongole.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Spaghetti_alle_vongole.jpg "fig:Spaghetti_alle_vongole.jpg") [Spaghetti_mit_Miesmuscheln.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Spaghetti_mit_Miesmuscheln.jpg "fig:Spaghetti_mit_Miesmuscheln.jpg") [Spaghetti](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaghetti "wikilink"), *[linguine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/linguine "wikilink")* and *paccheri* match very well with fish and seafood. From this union come the dishes typical of important lunches or dinners (weddings, in particular). The most typical ones are:

  - *[Spaghetti alle vongole](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaghetti_alle_vongole "wikilink")* or other shellfishes ([clam](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/clam "wikilink")s, [mussel](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/mussel "wikilink")s, and other)
  - *Paccheri con la zuppa di pesce* (*[scorfani](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpaena "wikilink")*, *[cuocci](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_robin "wikilink")*, *[tracine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weever "wikilink")* and more)
  - *Pasta con i calamari*, with [squid](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/squid "wikilink") sauce, cooked with white wine

There are many more varieties, for instance spaghetti with a white sauce of mediterranean [cod](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cod "wikilink").

Sometimes the traditional dishes of pasta with legumes can be mixed with seafood, so there are, for instance, *pasta e fagioli con le cozze* (pasta with beans and mussels), or other more modern variations, like pasta with zucchine and [clam](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/clam "wikilink")s, that lose any traditional connotation.

## Rice dishes

The most famous rice dish is the *[sartù di riso](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/sartù_di_riso "wikilink")*, a sort of *[timballo](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/timballo "wikilink")* made with rice, stuffed with chicken livers, [sausage](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/sausage "wikilink"), little [meatball](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/meatball "wikilink")s, *fior di latte* or *provola*, [pea](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pea "wikilink")s, mushrooms, and with Neapolitan ragù, or, in the white version (*"in bianco"*) with [béchamel sauce](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/béchamel_sauce "wikilink").

In the cuisine of the poor, rice is also cooked as *riso e verza* (rice with [cabbage](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cabbage "wikilink")), flavored with little pieces of [parmigiano-reggiano](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/parmigiano-reggiano "wikilink") cheese crusts, that slightly melt while cooking.

A seafood rice dish is the *risotto alla pescatora* ("Fisherman's [risotto](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/risotto "wikilink")"), prepared with various mollusks (different types of clams, squids, cuttlefishes), shrimps and a broth made from the boiling of seafood shells.

The [Arancini](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arancini "wikilink") (*palle 'e riso*), more typical of Sicilian cuisine, are also frequently used in Naples.

## Pizza

[Eq_it-na_pizza-margherita_sep2005_sml.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Eq_it-na_pizza-margherita_sep2005_sml.jpg "fig:Eq_it-na_pizza-margherita_sep2005_sml.jpg")\]\]

[Pizza](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pizza "wikilink") is the most popular and best known creation of all Neapolitan cuisine. It soon became very popular among the people as well as barons or princes: it was present in the [Bourbon](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Bourbon "wikilink") court. [King Ferdinand I](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_the_Two_Sicilies "wikilink") experienced cooking pizza in Capodimonte's porcelain ovens. After [Italian unification](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_unification "wikilink"), the new kings were also attracted by this southern food. The *pizzaiolo* [Raffaele Esposito](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raffaele_Esposito "wikilink") created in 1889, in honour to queen [Margherita of Savoy](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margherita_of_Savoy "wikilink") a nationalistic pizza, where the colours of the Italian flag were represented by the mozzarella (white), tomato (red) and basil (green). Since then this pizza is called the *pizza Margherita*. Pizza can be cheap and nutritious, so it had great success very quickly. Sometimes pizza is made in home ovens, but the real Neapolitan pizza must be cooked in a [wood-fired oven](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/wood-fired_oven "wikilink"), hand-made by an able *pizzaiolo* who makes the dough disk thinner in the centre and thicker in the outer part; the ingredients and olive oil are rapidly spread on the disk, and with a quick movement the pizza is put on the shovel and then slid in the oven where it is turned around a few times for uniform cooking.

## Fish and seafood dishes

One of the most famous main courses is a seafood dish recipe coming from the quarter 'Santa Lucia': *polpi alla luciana*, [octopus](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/octopus "wikilink") cooked with [chili pepper](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chili_pepper "wikilink") and tomato. [Octopus](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octopus "wikilink") is also simply steamed, and prepared as salad with lemon juice, parsley and green olives. A richer seafood salad can be prepared also mixing [squid](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/squid "wikilink"), [cuttlefish](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cuttlefish "wikilink") and [prawn](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/prawn "wikilink")s.

Medium size fishes are cooked *all'acqua pazza*, with tomato, garlic and parsley; the larger ones are simply grilled, accompanied, in the most important meals, with king size [prawn](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/prawn "wikilink")s.

[Mussel](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mussel "wikilink")s are prepared in different ways: rapidly steamed with black pepper (*all'impepata*), and dressed with a few drops of lemon juice each; also cooked *al [gratin](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/gratin "wikilink")*. Clams and other shellfishes are also cooked *[sauté](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sautéing "wikilink")*, rapidly passed in a large pan with olive oil, garlic, and served on crust breads.

Cheap fish can also produce very tasty recipes. The most popular one is [anchovy](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/anchovy "wikilink"). The best recipes are:

  - *Alici dorate e fritte*, boneless anchovies, passed in [flour](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/flour "wikilink"), egg and deep-fried
  - **, raw anchovies marinated in lemon juice or vinegar, then dressed with olive oil, garlic and parsley
  - *Alici arreganate*, boneless anchovies, rapidly cooked in a large pan with olive oil, lemon juice and [origanum](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/origanum "wikilink")

*Cicenielli*, the tiny baby fishes, are either steamed and dressed with oil and lemon, or deep-fried in a light dough, which is also used to deep-fry little pieces of some sea algae.

The *frittura di paranza* (deep-fried fishes) is usually done with small-sized local fishes, like [cod](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cod "wikilink"), [goatfish](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/goatfish "wikilink"), [anchovies](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anchovy "wikilink") and others. It should be eaten very hot, right after being fried (*frijenno magnanno*). Baby shrimps, sold alive, are fried with no flour, unlike the *paranza*.

## Vegetable dishes

[Melanzane_alla_parmigiana.jpg](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Melanzane_alla_parmigiana.jpg "fig:Melanzane_alla_parmigiana.jpg")\]\]

Vegetable dishes can become very rich and elaborated. The most famous are:

  - The *[parmigiana di melanzane](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/parmigiana_di_melanzane "wikilink")*, [aubergine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/aubergine "wikilink") pie with tomato sauce and [fiordilatte](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/fiordilatte "wikilink")
  - The **, potato pie stuffed with cheese and salami
  - The *peperoni ripieni*, stuffed whole peppers
  - The *melanzane a barchetta*, [aubergine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/aubergine "wikilink")s cut in half, the center scooped out and filled with different types of stuffing.

## Fried food

Fried fish was already mentioned above in the text; many vegetables are deep-fried with flour and egg (*dorate e fritte*): [artichoke](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/artichoke "wikilink"), zucchini, [cauliflower](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cauliflower "wikilink"). The richest version add pieces of liver, [ricotta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ricotta "wikilink") and, in the past, cow's brain. [Mozzarella](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozzarella "wikilink") can be prepared *dorata e fritta* as well and also *in carrozza*, passed in flour and egg together with two bread slices softened in [milk](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/milk "wikilink"), to form a small sandwich. Typical Neapolitan fried food are also the *[crocchè](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/crocchè "wikilink")*, stuffed potato balls passed in [breadcrumb](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/breadcrumb "wikilink")s and deep fried, or also the **, zucchini's male flowers fried in a dough, that can also be bought on the streets of Naples historical center in typical fried food shops, called [friggitoria](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/friggitoria "wikilink"), together with  (fried slices of [polenta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/polenta "wikilink")),  (fried bread dough balls) and [aubergine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/aubergine "wikilink") slices.

Onions, fried up to a golden color, are the base for the famous *[frittata](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/frittata "wikilink") di cipolle* (onion [omelette](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/omelette "wikilink")).

## Side-dishes

After pasta, the main second-course meals are frequently accompanied by side-dishes. The most popular ones are:

  - **, deep fried sliced zucchini dressed with [vinegar](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/vinegar "wikilink") and fresh [mint](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/mentha "wikilink")
  - **, fried [aubergine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/aubergine "wikilink")s, in two versions: stick-shaped and fried, then dressed with cherry tomato sauce, or dice-fried, with no tomato
  - *Peperoni in padella*, sliced peppers pan fried with black [Gaeta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaeta "wikilink") olives and [caper](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/caper "wikilink")s
  - *Peperoncini verdi fritti*, local small non-spicy green peppers, dressed with cherry tomato sauce
  - *[Friarielli](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friarielli "wikilink")*, local vegetable leaves, pan fried with oil, garlic and chili pepper. They often are side-dishes of fried [sausage](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/sausage "wikilink")s and , which are sometimes also accompanied by potato fries, typically cut as small dices

## Savory pies

Savory pies are convenient for outdoor food. The most popular savory pies are:

Those vegetables are the stuffing for the pie, which is made with a simple dough of flour, water and [yeast](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/yeast "wikilink")
  - The **, or *tortano*, typical of Easter holidays, usually prepared for the day after Easter, usually spent outdoor

## Holiday food

[Struffoli.JPG](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Struffoli.JPG "fig:Struffoli.JPG")

Holiday recipes deserve a dedicated section because of their variety and richness.

### Christmas

Christmas Eve dinner is usually the time when all family members join. It is typically done with [spaghetti alle vongole](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/spaghetti_alle_vongole "wikilink") followed by *capitone fritto* and *[baccalà](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/baccalà "wikilink") fritto* (deep fried [eel](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eel_\(food\) "wikilink") and [stockfish](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/stockfish "wikilink")); as a side-dish there is the **, a salad made with steamed [cauliflower](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cauliflower "wikilink"), [giardiniera](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/giardiniera "wikilink"), spicy and sweet peppers (*pupaccelle*), olives and [anchovies](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anchovy_\(food\) "wikilink"), all dressed with oil and [vinegar](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/vinegar "wikilink").

Christmas cakes are:

  - *[Struffoli](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Struffoli "wikilink")*
  - **
  - *[Mustacciuoli](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mustacciuoli "wikilink")*
  - **

Christmas Eve dinner is completed with the *ciociole*, which are dried fruits ([walnut](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/walnut "wikilink")s, [hazelnut](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/hazelnut "wikilink")s and [almond](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/almond "wikilink")s), dried [figs](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_fig "wikilink") and the *castagne del prete*, baked [chestnut](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chestnut "wikilink")s.

Christmas lunch has typically the *[minestra maritata](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/minestra_maritata "wikilink")* or hand-made pasta with chicken broth.

### Easter food

[Pastiera_Napoletana.JPG](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Pastiera_Napoletana.JPG "fig:Pastiera_Napoletana.JPG")

The main [Easter](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Easter "wikilink") dishes are the ** or *tortano*, a salty pie made with bread dough stuffed with various types of salami and cheese, also used the day after Easter for outdoor lunches. Typical of Easter lunches and dinners is the *fellata*, a banquet of [salami](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/salami "wikilink") and [capocollo](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/capocollo "wikilink") and salty [ricotta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ricotta "wikilink"). Typical dishes are also [lamb](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton "wikilink") or [goat](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/goat "wikilink") baked with potatoes and peas. Easter cake is the *[pastiera](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pastiera "wikilink")*.

### Other holidays

[Carnival](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnival "wikilink") has the Neapolitan version of *[lasagna](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/lasagna "wikilink")*, that has no [béchamel sauce](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/béchamel_sauce "wikilink"), unlike other Italian versions. As dessert, there is the *[Sanguinaccio dolce](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanguinaccio_dolce "wikilink")* with *[savoiardi](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/savoiardi "wikilink")* biscuits, or also the *[chiacchiere](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chiacchiere "wikilink")*, diffused all over Italy with different names.

2 November ([All Souls Day](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_Souls_Day "wikilink")) cake is the *torrone dei morti*, which, unlike the usual *[torrone](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/torrone "wikilink")* is not made with [honey](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/honey "wikilink") and [almond](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/almond "wikilink")s, but with [cocoa](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chocolate "wikilink") and a variety of suffings, like [hazelnut](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/hazelnut "wikilink")s, dried and candy fruits or also [coffee](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/coffee "wikilink") and more.

## Fruit

Fruit is often present at the end of a meal. Local production is abundant, one of the most popular local products is the [annurca](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/annurca "wikilink") apple, a local type of [apple](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/apple "wikilink") whose origins are old indeed: it is believed to have first been planted by the Romans. Slices of [watermelon](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/watermelon "wikilink") ('' 'o mellone*) were in old times sold in little street shops (*mellunari*), nowadays disappeared. The sweet and tasty yellow [peach](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/peach "wikilink") (*'o percuoco c' 'o pizzo'', in [Neapolitan](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neapolitan_language "wikilink")) is also sometimes used, chopped in pieces to add flavor to red wine coming from [Monte di Procida](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monte_di_Procida "wikilink"), cold and somewhat similar to Spanish [sangria](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/sangria "wikilink").

## Wine

Many [wine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/wine "wikilink")s from [Campania](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campania "wikilink") match very well to the local cuisine. Among white wines the most famous are [Greco di Tufo](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greco_di_Tufo "wikilink"), [Falanghina](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campi_Flegrei_Falanghina "wikilink"), [Fiano di Avellino](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiano_di_Avellino "wikilink") and Asprinio di Aversa, while the most famous red wines are [Aglianico](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aglianico "wikilink"), [Taurasi DOCG](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurasi_DOCG "wikilink"),  also known as *pere 'e palummo*, [Solopaca](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solopaca "wikilink"), [Lacryma Christi](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacryma_Christi "wikilink") from [Vesuvius](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vesuvius "wikilink"), that is produced both white and red.

## Liqueurs

The most abundant lunches or dinners end with [coffee](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/coffee "wikilink") and [liqueur](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/liqueur "wikilink"). *[Limoncello](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limoncello "wikilink")* is now world-famous, but once upon a time the most preferred one was the *liquore ai quattro frutti*, with [lemon](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/lemon "wikilink"), [orange](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/orange_\(fruit\) "wikilink"), [tangerine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/tangerine "wikilink") e *limo* (not to be confused with [lime](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lime_\(fruit\) "wikilink")), which is a local variation of [bergamot orange](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/bergamot_orange "wikilink"), now very rare. *[Nocillo](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nocillo "wikilink")* is also very popular all over Italy, and is the most appreciated bitter liqueur.

## Neapolitan street food

In Naples, the use of buying and eating food in the streets dates to very ancient times. The origins probably date back to Roman *[thermopolia](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/thermopolium "wikilink")* or maybe earlier. Typical fried food can still today be bought in little shops, like  (deep fried bread dough balls),  (deep fried [polenta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/polenta "wikilink") slices) and  (deep fried male [zucchini](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/zucchini "wikilink") flowers), or deep fried [aubergine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/aubergine "wikilink")s. [Pizza](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pizza "wikilink") is also prepared in small sizes to be eaten in the street, the so-called *pizza a libretto*, still found in Naples pizzerias in via dei Tribunali, port'Alba and piazza Cavour. In via Pignasecca, in the historical center, there are still some *carnacuttari* shops, selling various types of [tripe](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/tripe "wikilink"), ** (pork's foot and cow's nose) or the old *zuppa 'e carnacotta* ([tripe](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/tripe "wikilink") soup).

From [Mergellina](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mergellina "wikilink") to via Caracciolo there are still several little shops selling *[taralli](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/taralli "wikilink") nzogna e pepe* (salty biscuits with pork's fat and black pepper). Nowadays the old typical '' 'o broro 'e purpo'' ([octopus](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/octopus "wikilink") broth) has become extremely rare to find. A few decades ago, street shops sold *'o spassatiempo*, a mix of baked [hazelnut](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/hazelnut "wikilink")s, [pumpkin](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/pumpkin "wikilink") seeds, toasted [chickpea](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/chickpea "wikilink")s and [lupin](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/lupin "wikilink")s under [brine](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/brine "wikilink").

### Fusion cuisine

Many Neapolitan cookery books report classic recipes, but also re-interpretations in Neapolitan style of other ricette. So, it is not unusual to find recipes like *[cotoletta](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/cotoletta "wikilink") alla milanese*, *carne alla genovese*, *sugo alla bolognese*, and other. Books with both classic and revisited recipes are:

  - Jeanne Caròla Francesconi, *La vera cucina di Napoli*, edit. Newton, 1995, ()
  - *Frijenno magnanno*, Salvatore di Fraia Editore, [Pozzuoli](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pozzuoli "wikilink") (NA): Contains a large variety of recipes and creative neapolitan dishes.
