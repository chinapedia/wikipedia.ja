[数学](../Page/数学.md "wikilink")において**[体論](../Page/体論.md "wikilink")**とは、[体を研究する分野のことである](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/可換体 "wikilink")。これは体論における用語の一覧である。

## 体の定義

**体**とは、0 でない全ての元が積に関して逆元を持つ[環のこと](../Page/環論.md "wikilink")。体の上では、[四則演算](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/四則演算 "wikilink")が出来る。

体 *F* から 0 を除いた[群を](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/群_\(数学\) "wikilink") *F*<sup>×</sup> と書く

*F* に係数をもつ [多項式環](../Page/多項式環.md "wikilink") (*polynomial ring*) は、 *F*\[*x*\] と書く

  - **[標数](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/標数 "wikilink")** ([w:Characteristic (algebra)](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/w:Characteristic_\(algebra\) "wikilink")) 体*F*の標数とは非負の整数*n*であって、*F*において*n*個の1を足し合わせると0と等しくなる（1+1+ ... +1 = 0）ような*n*のうち、最小のものである。そのような*n*が存在しないときは*n* = 0と定める。

## 基本的な定義

  - [部分体](../Page/体の拡大.md "wikilink") (*Subfield*) \<\!-- : A *subfield* of a field *F* is a [subset](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/subset "wikilink") of *F* which is closed under the field operation + and \* of *F* and which, with these operations, forms itself a field.

\--\>

  - [素体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/素体 "wikilink") (*Prime field*)\<\!-- : A *prime field* is the unique smallest subfield of *F*.

\--\>

  - [拡大体](../Page/体の拡大.md "wikilink") (*Extension field*)\<\!-- : If *F* is a subfield of *E* then *E* is an extension field of *F*.

\--\>

  - [代数拡大](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/代数拡大 "wikilink") (*Algebraic extension*) \<\!-- : If an element α of an extension field *E* over *F* is the [root](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/root "wikilink") of a polynomial in *F*\[*x*\], then α is *algebraic* over *F*. If every element of *E* is algebraic over *F*, then *E* is an *algebraic extension* of *F*.

\--\>

  - [分解体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/分解体 "wikilink") (*Splitting field*)\<\!-- : A field extension generated by the complete factorisation of a polynomial.

\--\>

  - [正規拡大](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/正規拡大 "wikilink") (*Normal extension*)\<\!-- : A field extension generated by the complete factorisation of a set of polynomials.

\--\>

  - [分離拡大](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/分離拡大 "wikilink") (*Separable extension*)\<\!-- : An extension generated by roots of [separable polynomials](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/separable_polynomial "wikilink").

\--\>

  - [原始元](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/原始元_\(体論\) "wikilink") (*Primitive element*)\<\!-- : A element α of an extension field *E* over a field *F* is called a *primitive element* if *E*=*F*(α), the smallest extension field containing α.

\--\>

  - [完全体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/完全体 "wikilink") (*Perfect field*)\<\!-- : A field such that every finite extension is separable. All fields of characteristic zero, and all finite fields, are perfect.

\--\>

  - [代数的閉体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/代数的閉体 "wikilink") (*Algebraically closed field*)\<\!-- : A maximal algebraic extension field of *F* is its [algebraic closure](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/algebraic_closure "wikilink"). A field is algebraically closed if it is its own algebraic closure.

\--\>

  - [超越的](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/超越拡大 "wikilink") (*Transcendental*)\<\!-- : If an element is not algebraic over *F*, then it is *transcendental*.

\--\>

  - [超越次数](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/超越次数 "wikilink") (*Transcendence degree*)\<\!-- : The number of independent transcendental elements in a field extension. It is used to define the [dimension of an algebraic variety](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/dimension_of_an_algebraic_variety "wikilink").

\--\>

## 準同型

  - [体準同型](../Page/準同型.md "wikilink") (*Field homomorphism*)\<\!-- : A *field homomorphism* between two fields *E* and *F* is a [function](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/function_\(mathematics\) "wikilink")

<!-- end list -->

  -

      -
        *f* : *E* → *F*

    such that

      -
        *f*(*x* + *y*) = *f*(*x*) + *f*(*y*)

    and

      -
        *f*(*xy*) = *f*(*x*) *f*(*y*)

    for all *x*, *y* in *E*, as well as *f*(1) = 1. These properties imply that *f*(0) = 0, *f*(*x*<sup>-1</sup>) = *f*(*x*)<sup>-1</sup> for *x* in *E* with *x* ≠ 0, and that *f* is [injective](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/injective "wikilink"). Fields, together with these homomorphisms, form a [category](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/category_theory "wikilink"). Two fields *E* and *F* are called **isomorphic** if there exists a [bijective](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/bijective "wikilink") homomorphism

      -
        *f* : *E* → *F*.

    The two fields are then identical for all practical purposes; however, not necessarily in a *unique* way. See, for example, [complex conjugation](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/complex_conjugation "wikilink").

\--\>

## 体の種類

  - [有限体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/有限体 "wikilink") (*Finite field*)\<\!-- : 要素が有限個しかない体

\--\>

  - [順序体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/順序体 "wikilink") (*Ordered field*)\<\!-- : A field with a [total order](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/total_order "wikilink") compatible with its operations.

\--\>

  - [有理数](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/有理数 "wikilink")体 (*Field of rational numbers*)
  - [実数](../Page/実数.md "wikilink")体 (*Field of real numbers*)
  - [複素数](../Page/複素数.md "wikilink")体 (*Field of complex numbers*)
  - [数体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/数体 "wikilink") (*Number field*)\<\!-- : Algebraic extension of the field of rational numbers.

\--\>

  - [代数的数](../Page/代数的数.md "wikilink")体 (*Field of algebraic numbers*)\<\!-- : The field of algebraic numbers is the smallest algebraically closed extension of the field of rational numbers. Their detailed properties are studied in [algebraic number theory](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/algebraic_number_theory "wikilink").

\--\>

  - [二次体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/二次体 "wikilink") (*Quadratic field*) \<\!-- : A degree-two extension of the rational numbers.

\--\>

  - [円分体](../Page/円分体.md "wikilink") (*Cyclotomic field*)\<\!-- : An extension of the rational numbers generated by a [root of unity](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/root_of_unity "wikilink").

\--\>

  - [総実体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/総実体 "wikilink") (*Totally real field*)\<\!-- : A number field generated by a root of a polynomial, having all its roots real numbers.

\--\>

  - （形式的）[実体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/実体_\(数学\) "wikilink") (*Formally real field*)
  - [実閉体](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/実閉体 "wikilink") (*Real closed field*)

## ガロア理論

  - [ガロア拡大](../Page/ガロア拡大.md "wikilink") (*Galois extension*)\<\!-- : A normal, separable field extension.

\--\>

  - [ガロア群](../Page/ガロア群.md "wikilink") (*Galois group*)\<\!-- : The [automorphism group](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/automorphism_group "wikilink") of a Galois extension. When it is a finite extension, this is a finite group of order equal to the degree of the extension. Galois groups for infinite extensions are [profinite groups](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/profinite_group "wikilink").

\--\>

  - [クンマー理論](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/クンマー理論 "wikilink") (*Kummer theory*)\<\!-- : The Galois theory of taking *n*-th roots, given enough [roots of unity](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/roots_of_unity "wikilink"). It includes the general theory of [quadratic extensions](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/quadratic_extension "wikilink").

\--\>

  - [アルティン=シュライアー理論](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/アルティン=シュライアー理論 "wikilink") (*Artin-Scheier theory*)\<\!-- : Covers an exceptional case of Kummer theory, in characteristic *p*.

\--\>

  - [正規基底](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/正規基底 "wikilink") (*Normal basis*)\<\!-- : A basis in the vector space sense of *L* over *K*, on which the Galois group of *L* over *K* acts transitively.

\--\>

  - [体のテンソル積](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/体のテンソル積 "wikilink") (*Tensor product of fields*)\<\!-- : A different foundational piece of algebra, including the [compositum](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/compositum "wikilink") operation ([join](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/join "wikilink") of fields).

\--\>

## ガロア理論周辺

  - [ガロア理論の逆問題](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/ガロア理論の逆問題 "wikilink") (*Inverse problem of Galois theory*)\<\!-- : Given a group *G*, find an extension of the rational number or other field with *G* as Galois group.

\--\>

  - [微分ガロア理論](../Page/微分ガロア理論.md "wikilink") (*Differential Galois theory*)\<\!-- : The subject in which symmetry groups of [differential equations](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/differential_equation "wikilink") are studied along the lines traditional in Galois theory. This is actually an old idea, and one of the motivations when [Sophus Lie](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sophus_Lie "wikilink") founded the theory of [Lie groups](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lie_group "wikilink"). It has not, probably, reached definitive form.

\--\>

  - [グロタンディークのガロア理論](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/グロタンディークのガロア理論 "wikilink") (*Grothendieck's Galois theory*)\<\!-- : A very abstract approach from [algebraic geometry](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/algebraic_geometry "wikilink"), introduced to study the analogue of the [fundamental group](https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/fundamental_group "wikilink").

\--\>
